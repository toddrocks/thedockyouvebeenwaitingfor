The waterfront

There are two kinds of waterfront. This is the kind you live on.

Roughly 130 feet of the property's southern edge is Choctawhatchee Bay — open, deep, facing due south. That orientation is the whole game: sun on the water from morning to evening, with both the sunrise and the sunset playing out across the bay instead of behind a neighbor's roofline.

01

Deepwater, on your schedule

The lift sits over water deep enough to actually use — no standing around waiting on the tide before you can go fishing.

02

Fish-cleaning station & dockside shower

Catch dinner, clean it, and rinse off the evidence without ever setting foot on dry land. Yes — a shower, on the dock.

03

Sundeck at the end of the run

A covered platform out over the bay — the best seat on the property for weather coming across the water.

04

Seawall & rip rap

A vinyl seawall and rip rap hold the shoreline in place — the unromantic infrastructure that keeps waterfront homes from quietly becoming underwater ones.

05

Sunrise and sunset, both on the water

The due-south exposure means the sky puts on two shows a day — and the bay hands both of them back in reflection.

06

Dolphins in the bay

Choctawhatchee Bay's dolphins work this stretch of water. Some mornings the show simply comes to you.

07

Detached garage, workshop & carport

A detached 2-car garage, a separate storage building and workshop, plus a carport — room for every boat, trailer, and toy the water invites.

08

Nearly an acre, level

0.9 of an acre of usable, level ground — rare on the water, with room to build, add, or simply spread out.

09

Freeport — and climbing

One of the Emerald Coast's fastest-growing communities, minutes from 30A and the bay's best water — a location moving in the right direction.

Life on the water

Not staged. Just Tuesdays here.

These aren't listing photos — they're snapshots the current owners took from this dock. Redfish and speckled trout that never saw a boat, and the kind of evenings that show up for free. This is what the place actually is when no one's trying to sell it.

The land & the house

Nearly an acre, level, and honest about what it is.

The home is a solid all-brick ranch — aged gracefully and updated where it counts, with vaulted ceilings and a brick fireplace. It won't win a magazine cover, and it doesn't need to: it's clean, comfortable, and move-in ready, on a lot that's the real prize.

For everything that refuses to live indoors — boats, kayaks, and the water toys that multiply once you live somewhere like this — there's a carport, a detached 2-car garage, and a separate storage building and workshop.
