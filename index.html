The waterfront

There are two kinds of waterfront. This is the kind you live on.

Roughly 130 feet of the property's southern edge is Choctawhatchee Bay — open, deep, facing due south. That orientation is the whole game: sun on the water from morning to evening, with both the sunrise and the sunset playing out across the bay instead of behind a neighbor's roofline.

The land & the house

Nearly an acre, level, and honest about what it is.

The home is a solid all-brick ranch — aged gracefully and updated where it counts, with vaulted ceilings and a brick fireplace. It won't win a magazine cover, and it doesn't need to: it's clean, comfortable, and move-in ready, on a lot that's the real prize.

For everything that refuses to live indoors — boats, kayaks, and the water toys that multiply once you live somewhere like this — there's a carport, a detached 2-car garage, and a separate storage building and workshop.
